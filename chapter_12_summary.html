Chapter 12: Performance Evaluation

  • Controllable Procurement and Manufacturing Costs to Revenue
  • The FI Index allows for easy comparison of firms within the same industry, facilitating informed investment decisions.
  • FLEXEE Simulation: A comprehensive tool that simulates the management of supply chain operations, allowing users to make strategic decisions in a controlled environment.
  • Supply Chain: The network of suppliers, manufacturers, warehouses, distribution centers, and retailers that work together to produce and deliver products to consumers.
  • Production Strategy: The approach a company takes to manufacturing goods, focusing on factors such as cost efficiency, quality control, and time management.
  • Postponed Production: A manufacturing strategy where the final assembly of products is delayed until there is a confirmed demand, minimizing inventory costs and improving responsiveness to customer needs.
  • Subassemblies: Components that are pre-assembled as part of a larger product, such as a car engine or computer motherboard, before being integrated into the final product.
  • Raw Materials: The basic materials used to create products, such as metals, plastics, and chemicals, often sourced from suppliers before being processed in manufacturing.
  • Procurement: The process of acquiring raw materials, parts, and services from external suppliers to be used in the production process.
  • Inventory Management: The oversight of storing and managing the flow of raw materials, work-in- progress, and finished goods to ensure that production runs smoothly and customer orders are fulfilled.
