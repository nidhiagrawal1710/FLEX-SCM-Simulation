Chapter 9: Forecasting Decisions

  • Quarterly sales forecasts are required for each product, region, and channel.
  • Forecasting accuracy directly affects administrative overhead: 1% inaccuracy = 1% cost increase.
  • Maximum penalty: overhead can double with poor forecasting.
  • Forecast error costs are recorded in the P&L as 'Forecast Inaccuracy'.
  • Accurate forecasting improves operational efficiency and lowers inventory imbalance risk.
  • Forecasting formula: Accuracy = 100 Ã— (1 - |Forecast - Actual| / Actual)
  • Forecasting affects both direct cost penalties and long-term performance evaluation scores
